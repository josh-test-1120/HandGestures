Detection of Epileptic Seizures
Through Motion Recognition
Using Machine Learning

Our Project at a Glance: Why it matters: Epileptic seizures can happen suddenly and pose serious risks if not quickly recognized, yet current solutions often rely on cameras or bulky devices that affect comfort or privacy. What we're doing: We use small wearable motion sensors and a room-mounted ultrasonic device to detect seizure-related movements like tremors, stiffening, and posture changes. Our goal: To create a smart, real-time detection system that protects privacy and displays results through an easy-to-use website, supporting early intervention at home or in clinical settings.

Who We Are

Brought together by a shared passion for innovation and impact, we are a team of five driven CWU Des Moines students who first met in CS325 (Technical Writing for Computer Science) during Fall 2023. What began as classroom collaboration quickly evolved into a mission—to harness our combined strengths in hardware and software development to address a real-world challenge.

With guidance from Dr. Zhu and Dr. Gao, we set out to design a system that empowers individuals living with epilepsy through real-time motion-based monitoring. This work represents more than just research; it’s our commitment to creating technology that protects, uplifts, and respects the dignity of every person it serves.

How It Works

From motion sensing to intelligent prediction, here's how our system functions step by step.

Arduino Hardware

Custom-built Arduino system with precision IMU sensors to track motion and acceleration.

Motion Detection

Real-time monitoring algorithms detect movement patterns related to seizure activity.

Data Analysis

Machine learning models analyze sensor data to predict and flag early seizure indicators.
